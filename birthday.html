Where it began

The Road That Led Here

Day 1–15

The staring

Two strangers, the same stretch of road, the same time every morning. Neither of us said a word — we just kept looking. Fifteen days of pretending not to notice each other, while noticing everything.

The turning point

A wave, and a smile

Then one day, out of nowhere, a hand went up. And you smiled back. That one wave undid two weeks of silence.

Soon after

Instagram IDs, and then, everything

An Instagram handle exchanged on the road turned into messages, into calls, into conversations that didn't want to end.

And then

Meetings, roaming, and us

Meeting up. Roaming around with no real destination, just an excuse to be near each other. Somewhere in all that aimless wandering, we stopped being strangers and started being us.

The real part

Fights, misunderstandings, and staying anyway

It wasn't always easy. There were fights, there were misunderstandings, there were days we didn't get each other at all. But we stayed. We chose each other, again and again.

Today

A lot of love

And after all of it — the staring, the wave, the roaming, the fights — what's left standing is just a lot of love. That's the whole story, really.

For you

A Letter To My Sweet Potato

My Shweta,

I still think about those fifteen days on the road — how neither of us had the courage to say anything, how we just kept stealing glances and pretending we weren't. I didn't know it then, but that was the beginning of the best thing in my life.

You waved, you smiled, and somehow that small moment changed everything after it. The messages, the calls, the roaming around with no plan — I wasn't falling for a destination, I was falling for you.

We've had our fights. We've misunderstood each other more times than I can count. But every single time, what brought us back was never doubt — it was love, quietly waiting for us to stop being stubborn.

Today is your day, sweet potato. I hope it reminds you how loved you are — not just today, but on the ordinary days too. The staring-on-the-road days, the fighting days, the roaming days, all of it.

Happy birthday. Here's to more roads, more waves, and a lot more love.

— always yours
